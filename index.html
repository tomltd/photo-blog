Canada 2015

We travelled to British Columbia in June 2015, after spending a few days seeing the sights of Vancouver we hired a car drove up the coast via Whistler then across country to the Alberta rockies.

This Black bear was hanging out in Jasper National Park. The bears in the National Parks are very mild manored as they are used to the tourist traffic. In total we spotted 8 bears and had a close encounter with a couple of bears who were having a picnic in the woods.

The shot above was taken from Mount Seymour just outside Vancouver. It's so nice to be half an hours drive from some beautiful scenery.

Back to Jasper National Park. This shot was taken during a hike up the Sulphur Skyline trail. At the top we were greeted with an amazing view, some mountain goats and a few friendly Chipmunks.

We were warned the area is prone to afternoon storms and it did not disappoint. You can see a storm coming in down the valley in the distance

New Zealand 2013

In May 2013 we visited some relatives living in New Zealand. The scenery is not too dissimilar to Canada, although you don't have to worry about bear and cougar attacks. The most dangerous animal is probably this friendly Kea. The Kea is the worlds only mountain parrot with a reputation for being a bit of a pick pocket. This one tried to steal my shoe right off my foot!

Here is a view from the Lake Tekapo observatory. One of the best things to see is the amazing night sky. Due to it's tiny population there is practically no light pollution.

Namibia 2010

In December 2010 we visited Namibia. This is another country with a tiny population to area ratio and also great for looking at the night sky. Above you can see a heard of tasty springbok. One thing not to be missed is the vast range of game meats to try.

I took this photo ib a boat trip at Walvis bay. These guys flew right past the boat. Other things we saw included a friendly seal who jumped on board and the worlds largest diamond vacuum.

Out last night in Rundu and I managed to assemble everyone for a photo. Needless to say this took a few attempts but we got there in the end!
